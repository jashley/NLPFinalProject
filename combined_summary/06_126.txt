high court consideration of s 51(xxiiia) the bma case 28 in the bma case , the high court held that the prohibition against civil conscription in s 51(xxiiia) applied only to medical and dental services and not to the other elements of the section.
the regulation of the manner in which such services are provided is properly incidental to the commonwealth's power under s 51(xxiiia) to provide medical services and does not offend the constitutional prohibition against civil conscription.
