the giving of reasons is thus an aspect of judicial accountability, which was identified by his honour in soulemezis as the second of the three purposes served by the judicial duty of giving of reasons.
more importantly, the general duty to give reasons, and its rationale as including proper exercise of rights of appeal, is well established: see soulemezis v dudley (holdings) pty ltd (1987) 10 nswlr 247; beale v government insurance office (nsw) (1997) 25 mvr 373.
