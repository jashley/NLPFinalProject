hence, there was error on the part of the principal member in appointing a different member to constitute the tribunal.
in the section headed "the constitution of the tribunal", apart from the provisions dealing with the initial direction as to who is to constitute the tribunal for the purposes of a particular review, cl 20 provides: as a general rule, if a person has previously had a case reviewed by the tribunal, any new case, or any case remitted for reconsideration, in which that person is an applicant will be constituted to a member other than the member who had previously constituted the tribunal.
the "usual position [is] that remission to a differently constituted tribunal is the ordinary way to proceed": industry research and development board v imt ltd [2001] fca 85 at [40] .
