after referring to phillips v walsh (1990) 20 nswlr 206 (" phillips "), his honour noted (at [26]-[28]) that, in interarch , there was only one "short issue of law" advanced on the summary judgment application, that there was no real virtue in fresh proceedings, and that there was "no substantive advantage to be derived from requiring the institution of fresh proceedings".
