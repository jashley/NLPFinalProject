the tribunal made an adverse credibility finding and did not accept that the appellant had suffered any harm.
the tribunal did not accept that the appellant was a falun gong practitioner, that he was involved in asking the chinese government to provide what he had called a "sufficient political and legal system" in china or that he had political opinions different from the chinese authorities.
his honour held that the tribunal did not rely on any information provided by the appellant because the decision was based upon its dissatisfaction with the appellant's answers to its questions.
