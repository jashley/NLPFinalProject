background 1 before me there are two notices of motion.
order 28 r 3(1) federal court rules provides some guidance as to circumstances which may be taken into account in making an order, namely: (a) that an applicant is ordinarily resident outside australia; (b) that an applicant is suing, not for the applicant's own benefit, but for the benefit of some other person and the court has reason to believe that the applicant will be unable to pay the costs of the respondent if ordered to do so; (c) subject to subrule (2), that the address of the applicant is not stated or is incorrectly stated in the originating process; (d) that an applicant has changed address after the commencement of the proceeding in an attempt to avoid the consequences of the proceeding.
